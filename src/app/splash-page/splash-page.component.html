Crypto crash course

CryptoCurrencies have been around since 1980, believe it or not. While they have thrived in various subcultures, it didn’t become a mainstream trend until bitcoin made its appearance. If someone had put $1 into bitcoin in early July of 2010 and sold it today, they would have $13,750,000 in the bank.

While trading cryptocurrencies is profitable, there is another aspect to this new wave of digital assets that many were not aware of. It is just as profitable, but a little more predictable. This is called mining. While the term seems a little confusing at first it’s actually a pretty simple concept.

Mining computers serve as auditors who don’t require pay or rest. When a crypto transaction is conducted, there needs to be some verification that it has happened. These transactions are added to a ledger after being verified using a measurably difficult algorithm. This ledger is called a blockchain. Every mining computer has a copy of the blockchain, and they all make sure nothing changes that shouldn’t. As a reward for verifying these transactions the parties who run the mining machines get a portion of the currency they are mining.
